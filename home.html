Personas & Storyboarding: The Envirobrite LED Task Lamp

Context

We were tasked with finding an interface somewhere in public space and interviewing three users about it. Based on those interviews, we created two personas and a storyboard to illustrate the user journey of one of those personas. With this task, I decided to choose...[drumroll please] a lamp. Why? Well, first, it is a lamp I love to use. But second, there are a few things that frustrate me about the interface, and I was wondering if others felt the same way.

The Interface

The selected interface is the LED Task Lamp, a desk lamp that is present in a common space in my house.

It has several different modes, as well as a timer, and needs to be set up before it is used (as can be stored in a folded state when not in use).

User interviews

The users

Interviewed users included one undergraduate student, one graduate student and teaching assistant, and one professional teacher. None of the respondents had seen or used the interface before.

The questions

Behavioral questions

  1. What kind of work do you do?
    • All three said that they do a great deal of typing, writing, reading, and Zoom meeting while working.
  2. Where do you work best?
    • Respondents prefer to work at a desk, but two responded that they also sometimes work in “soft seating.”
  3. Describe your workspace.
    • All respondents described their desk area; only one mentioned using a lamp. They described having their computers, a notebook or other paper, and books around their workspace at any given time. One noted having a lamp beside them. One respondent later stated that they never work with a tabletop lamp, but do sometimes work with lamps by soft seating.
  4. What time of day do you prefer to do your work?
    • Two respondents noted a preference for daytime work, though all said that they do work in the evening. One respondent preferred evening work.
  5. What kind of lighting do you like best?
    • All respondents said they prefer natural light. When asked to describe what lighting they prefer for evening work, all three said warm light.

Interface questions

  1. What does each circle do? (Describe what you can do with the button).
    • All respondents correctly identified the power button. Two respondents correctly identified the Mode button; one hesitated but eventually settled on “quality of light.” Respondents hesitated upon identifying the 1H/2H buttons; all three eventually identified it correctly. The arrow buttons were identified by two respondents to change the brightness of the light; one respondent suggested that it would increase or decrease the amount of time for which the light would stay on.
  2. What lighting mode do you prefer?
    • Respondents did not need to be prompted to answer this question; all three were forthcoming about their preference for the most yellow or warm light.

When requested to set up the lamp, users paused to observe it before initially setting it up; one attempted first to stand it upside-down. One user did not initially plug the lamp in before trying to turn it on by pressing on the power button; the other two plugged the lamp in and then tried to turn it on. One user took a few seconds to find the power button; the other two found it immediately. Users were then asked to identify what they thought the buttons would do before pressing them. All users intuitively knew how to press the buttons. All three users tapped the Mode button several times while testing it; two did this immediately upon pressing it, while one paused to inspect the light difference.

Personas

Frantic Felix

Fig. 3: Felix is a student who is taking Zoom classes and consequently has been experiencing a lot of eye strain. He’s got a deadline tonight and wants to get back to work as soon as possible, but his eyes don’t feel super great at the moment, between his readings and his extended screen time.

Inquisitive Ira

Fig. 4: Ira is a teacher-in-training who is currently in graduate school and working part-time as part of their degree. They also tutor on the side. They have limited space in their current room, since they’re trying to save on rent, and are missing personal interactions with their students a lot.

Storyboard
